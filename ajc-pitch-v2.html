The Opportunity

157 Years of Trust. One Gap to Close.

AJC has something most media companies would kill for — a century and a half of credibility in one of America's most dynamic, fastest-growing cities. The journalism is real. The reporters are talented. The brand is genuine. The problem isn't what AJC produces. It's the distance between what AJC produces and the people who should be consuming it.

That gap is closeable. And the tools to close it aren't complicated — they're just underutilized. This pitch is about how to use them.

Context

You're Already Moving. This Builds on That.

The digital pivot is real. UATL and AJC Varsity are exactly the right instinct — niche brands that tap into high-intent communities rather than chasing broad reach. Going fully digital January 1st wasn't a retreat, it was a declaration. And Travis arriving from eight years at The Washington Post signals the organization is serious about what comes next.

The Starting Point

Digital subscriptions are up 28% year-over-year. Revenue is now split 50/50 between advertising and subscriptions. The foundation is there. What's needed now is the strategy that turns social reach into subscriber loyalty — and community into revenue.

Core Philosophy

People Don't Connect With Brands.
They Connect With People.

Every strategy in this pitch flows from one idea: put faces, personalities, and real moments behind the masthead. The Ticket didn't build one of the most loyal radio audiences in America by being professional. They built it by being real. Barstool didn't grow by being objective. They grew by having a point of view. AJC doesn't need to become either of those things — but it can learn from what they understood about human connection.

Credibility is AJC's moat. Human connection is the bridge that gets people across it. Right now, the bridge isn't built.

Initiative 01

Get Ready With Us — Reporter-Led Video Content

Before a reporter covers a major city council vote, a Hawks game, or a breaking story — document the preparation. GRWM-style video content. Meta glasses for passive, authentic behind-the-scenes footage. Day-in-the-life journalism that pulls the curtain back on what it actually takes to tell a story.

The primary audience is younger people who are curious about journalism as a career. The secondary effect is more powerful: when reporters become personalities with their own followings, audience loyalty attaches to people, not just a masthead. That's a retention and growth story that compounds over time.

Revenue Connection

Reporter personality content drives follower growth on platform accounts. Follower growth expands reach of subscription conversion campaigns. Social audiences are the top of the subscriber funnel.

Initiative 02 — Biggest Opportunity

Regional Accounts: The Gap No One Is Owning

Hyperlocal is where legacy media has consistently failed and where audience hunger is most acute. People in Cobb County care deeply about what happens in Cobb County. People in Cherokee want to know about Cherokee. AJC has the infrastructure, the credibility, and the distribution to own this — and no competitor is doing it at scale.

Initiative 03

Community Workshops — Build the Next Generation of Readers

Social media workshops for schools and working professionals. Online safety, platform growth fundamentals, content strategy basics. Packaged once, deployed repeatedly. This isn't a primary revenue play — it's a community trust play. The students who attend a workshop today are the subscribers of 2030. The brand impression is worth more than the ticket price.

Initiative 04

Stay Credible. Stay Current.

The standard doesn't drop — the aperture widens. When a story generates mass cultural momentum, AJC should own it fully. The Afroman news cycle is the model: a story with genuine cultural resonance that generated enormous organic reach. The right play is a long-form documentary treatment, repurposed into short-form TikToks and Reels, discussed on the podcast, covered with a local angle.

One story. Every format. Maximum reach. That's the distribution strategy for the content that people actually want to share.

Podcast Strategy

Two pods: Sports (primary revenue driver through advertising) and Lifestyle & Entertainment (cultural commentary, trending stories, local voices). Both serve younger audiences. Both generate advertising CPM. Both feed subscriber conversion.

Initiative 05

The Barstool Model — With a Credibility Wall

Affiliate partnerships with local and regional TikTokers and content creators. They create for their audience under AJC sponsorship. AJC gets content at reduced production cost. Influencers get resources and reach. The audience gets voices they already trust pointing them toward AJC.

The guardrail is simple and non-negotiable: sponsored content is clearly labeled. Editorial coverage remains fully independent. The credibility wall stays intact. The partnership lives entirely on the distribution side, never the editorial side.

For major events — World Cup, NBA playoffs, SEC championships — AJC hosts influencer events. Bring creators to the scene. Mix UGC with produced content. Flood pages simultaneously from multiple voices. The result is reach that no single account can generate alone.

Initiative 06

Sports Is the Front Door for Every Young Reader

WNBA. NBA. NFL. World Cup. College football. Sports is the single most reliable entry point for younger audiences — and Atlanta is a sports city. Sponsored influencers covering games and events. A dedicated sports podcast with advertising revenue. Influencer events built around Atlanta's biggest sporting moments.

The affiliate model works especially well here — let creators do what they do for their audience, under AJC's banner, with AJC's resources behind them. They create the reach. We create the credibility. Both sides win.

Initiative 07

Treat Every Dollar Like a Marketer Would

Great content that no one sees is just a tree falling in an empty forest. Every post needs a distribution strategy — platform-specific formatting, timing, hashtag architecture, and engagement response built in from the start. Right content. Right channel. Right audience. Right time.

And when influencers, community members, or local businesses work with AJC — the experience has to be exceptional. Customer service is a content strategy. When people feel genuinely taken care of, they talk about it. That earned media is worth more than any boosted post.
